# Types of Design Careers Explained

Design is a broad field with many different paths. When people first explore "types of design industries, they quickly realise that “designer” can mean many different things depending on the problems being solved.

Some designers focus on research and usability. Others work on branding, animation, physical products, or even code. Many modern design roles overlap, especially in digital product teams.

On [Path to Design](https://www.pathtodesign.com), designers have shared their journeys across many different design careers. The sections below explain what each role typically involves, using insights directly from designers featured on the platform.

## Product Designer

Product designers work across the lifecycle of digital products. They help teams understand user problems, design solutions, and collaborate with engineers and product managers to bring ideas to life.

Many product designers work end-to-end, from early research to final delivery. Based from the quotes below from product designers that have written in, their responsibilities vary widely.

> My job is really about understanding people—what they need, what frustrates them, and how to make their experience better. — Martin Agubatag

> I begin by understanding the problem, users, and context through interviews, data review, and stakeholder alignment. — Elena Molinari

> Often times, the job entails taking the messy ideas of non-designers and making them tangible and feasible. — Meghan Martin

## UI/UX Designer

UI/UX designers combine user experience thinking with interface design. Their responsibilities often include researching user behaviour, designing flows, and creating the visual interface of products.

> The role ranges widely — from understanding behaviour and what the user needs, to finding the perfect creative solution and visualising it. — Sofia Joelson

> I work with engineers, product managers, and fellow designers to design, test, and launch features. — Owen Hudock

## UX Researcher

UX researchers focus on understanding users and their behaviours. Their work helps teams design products that genuinely meet people's needs.

> To listen, to understand, to guide and to translate. — Eriol Fox

UX researchers often conduct interviews, usability testing, and behavioural research to uncover insights that inform product decisions.

## Final thoughts

There are many different **types of design industries**, and each role focuses on a different part of creating products, services, and experiences.

Some designers specialise deeply in one discipline. Others move between different **design careers** throughout their journey.

If you're exploring the design field, the best way to understand these roles is by learning directly from designers who are already doing the work.
